Chest

Overview

Your chest is home to essential muscles that support breathing and upper body strength. A strong chest contributes to overall stability and posture and is key for athletic performance.

Nutrition Tips

Focus on lean proteins, fruits, and vegetables to nourish your muscles. Foods such as chicken, tofu, and spinach can help with muscle repair and growth. Don’t forget to include healthy fats and whole grains to fuel your workouts.

Exercises

Push-ups

Start in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor, then push back up. Maintain a steady pace and proper form throughout.

Chest Flyes

Using dumbbells, lie on a bench with arms extended above your chest. Slowly lower your arms out to the sides while keeping a slight bend in the elbows. Bring the weights back together to complete one repetition.

Incline Press

Set an incline bench at about 30–45°. Using a barbell or dumbbells, press the weight upward while focusing on your chest muscles. Lower the weight slowly and repeat for 10–12 repetitions.
