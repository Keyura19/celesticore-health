Legs

Overview

Your legs carry you through life by supporting movement and stability. They include key muscle groups such as the quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves, all essential for everyday mobility and athletic performance.

Nutrition Tips

Fuel your legs with complex carbohydrates, proteins, and healthy fats. Foods like sweet potatoes, lean meats, and nuts provide the energy needed for powerful workouts. Stay hydrated to help muscle recovery.

Exercises

Squats

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and lower your body as if sitting in an imaginary chair. Keep your back straight and ensure your knees do not extend past your toes. Rise back up by engaging your leg muscles.

Lunges

Step forward with one leg, lowering your hips until both knees form a 90° angle. Keep your front knee aligned over your ankle. Push back to standing and alternate legs for balance.

Calf Raises

Stand with your heels hanging slightly off a step. Raise your heels by contracting your calves, hold at the top, then lower slowly. Perform 15–20 repetitions to build strength.
